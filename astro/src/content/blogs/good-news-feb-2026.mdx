We are bringing visibility to disabilities by spotlighting a few of our favorite uplifting news stories on disability inclusion and accessibility from last month!

**February 3rd**: A Reuters Institute report by William Kremer challenges mainstream journalism on the credibility of its claim to serve the public interest. Through extensive research and panel discussions across England and Wales, Kremer shows how people with learning disabilities and autism are excluded from meaningful access to news. The report reveals a blind spot in journalism and offers solutions on how public service media can become more inclusive, relevant, and useful.

Original article: [Creating News for (and with) People with Learning Disabilities](
https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/creating-news-and-people-learning-disabilities)


**February 4th**: Burbank is the first Southern California city to launch a revolutionary mountain trail accessibility program, giving visitors with disabilities and mobility challenges the chance to explore scenic mountain hiking trails using specialized all-terrain wheelchairs that are available to reserve for two-hour timeslots. Burbank has established several inclusive sports programs in recent years, and officials plan to continue expanding the city's adaptive recreation opportunities.

Original article: [Burbank Just Launched a Game-Changing Hiking Program, and it's Opening Trails to More People Than Ever](https://secretlosangeles.com/burbank-hiking-trail-accessibility-program/)


**February 17th**: In 2024, the U.S. Department of Justice signed a new rule mandating that public sector government websites and mobile apps must meet accessibility standards defined by Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act. While smaller public entities and special purpose districts have until April 26, 2027 to comply, the April 24, 2026 deadline for all other jurisdictions is fast approaching.

Original article: [April Deadline Approaching to Implement Mandatory ADA Standards for Local Government Websites and Apps](
https://mrsc.org/stay-informed/mrsc-insight/february-2026/ada-standards-websites-apps)


**February 17th**: The Museum of Disability History has reopened in a new, larger location at The Viscardi Center in Albertson, New York. The fully accessible 4,500-square-foot museum features thousands of artifacts, documents, and photos that highlight the history, struggles, and achievements of people with disabilities and the disability rights movement.

Original article: [Museum of Disability History Reopens in New Location](
https://enablingdevices.com/blog/museum-of-disability-history-reopens-in-new-location/)


**February 18th**: A nine-year-old boy with autism is helping first responders more effectively support the Virginia autism community. Ledger Mann and his father, Sergeant JT Mann of the Prince George County Police Department, started creating emergency kits filled with sensory-soothing items designed to comfort individuals with and without autism during stressful situations. Thanks to local donors, the father-son duo has already supplied kits beyond county limits and plans to continue expanding.

Original article: [Virginia Third Grader Creates Emergency Autism Kits for First Responders](
https://www.wtvr.com/news/local-news/emergency-autism-kits-feb-18-2026)
