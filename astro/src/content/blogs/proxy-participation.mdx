# Proxy Protests

I was fortunate enough to go to the [Design and Disability Exhibit](https://www.vam.ac.uk/exhibitions/design-and-disability?srsltid=AfmBOoq6FBL0IvsVfCvRsq1XA_wvjbO16-ZbNvJ7Pyv_eH3-XGUW6GK3) at the Victoria Alberts museum this past fall.  The exhibit was excellent and had a number of items worth more discussion but in our rapidly changing world, I want to call out the proxy protest exhibit.  People with disabilities make up a large portion of every country’s population but many cannot participate directly in civic actions like protests.  They end up sidelined and their lack of ability to participate is itself hidden. 

## Sample Heading

If you are organizing or attending an event that affects the lives and livelihood of people with disabilities (Hint: most of them do), consider encouraging proxy attendance for people with disabilities that prevent them attending. It’s pretty simple these days: A person attending the protest wears a mobile device with a zoom call to allow the individual who can’t attend to be present at the event virtually.

The website [proxyprotest.com](http://proxyprotest.com) has more information including suggestions on how to wear a mobile phone for best effect.
